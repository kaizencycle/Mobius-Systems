# Conflict of Interest Policy

## Disclosure Requirements

All Council and TSC members must disclose:

### Financial Interests
- Equity or compensation from LLM vendors
- Consulting relationships with governments
- Ownership in competing platforms
- Material financial interests in Mobius ecosystem

### Professional Relationships
- Employment with model providers
- Board seats at related organizations
- Advisory roles with potential conflicts
- Speaking/consulting arrangements

## Recusal Requirements

Members must recuse from votes where conflicts exist:

- **Technical decisions** affecting vendor selection
- **Economic decisions** where personal gain possible
- **Governance** involving their employer/client
- **License/policy** changes benefiting their organization

## Annual Statements

Required annually (published publicly):
- Updated COI disclosure
- Recusal log from past year
- Material changes in relationships

## Enforcement

Undisclosed conflicts discovered:
- First violation: Public disclosure + warning
- Second violation: Removal vote by remaining members
- Severe cases: Immediate removal (â…” vote)

## Examples of Conflicts

### Acceptable
- Using Mobius in your organization
- Speaking about Mobius at conferences
- Contributing code while employed elsewhere

### Requires Disclosure
- Receiving compensation from OpenAI/Anthropic/Google
- Consulting for governments on AI policy
- Equity in competing agent platforms

### Prohibited
- Taking bribes to influence decisions
- Using position for personal financial gain
- Steering contracts to personal businesses
- Deliberate capture attempts

## Philosophy

This policy preserves:
- **Neutrality**: No vendor favoritism
- **Trust**: Public confidence in governance
- **Integrity**: Alignment with Mobius principles
- **Transparency**: Open governance model
